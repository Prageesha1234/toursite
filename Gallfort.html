Galle Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with colonial charm

Discover Galle Fort

Galle Fort, in the Bay of Galle on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, was built first in 1588 by the Portuguese, then extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century. This historic fortress is a living monument, inhabited by a vibrant community within the great ramparts, massive bastions, and cobblestone streets.

About Galle Fort

The fort covers nearly 130 acres and contains architecture reflecting Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences. The walls of the fort are built of granite and coral, with some sections reaching up to 6 meters in thickness. Within the fort, you'll find charming colonial-era houses, museums, churches, boutique shops, and restaurants.

Did You Know?

Galle Fort was miraculously spared from the 2004 tsunami due to its massive walls, which diverted the waves around the fort, saving most of the structures inside.

Things to Do

  • Walk along the fort ramparts at sunset
  • Visit the Galle Lighthouse (Sri Lanka's oldest)
  • Explore the National Maritime Museum
  • Shop for souvenirs at the boutique stores
  • Visit the Dutch Reformed Church (built 1755)
  • Enjoy fresh seafood at beachside restaurants

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Galle Fort is from November to April when the weather is dry and sunny. Early morning or late afternoon visits are ideal to avoid the midday heat.

How to Get There

Galle is located 119km south of Colombo along the coastal highway. You can reach Galle by:

  • Train: Scenic 2-hour coastal train from Colombo (recommended)
  • Bus: Regular buses from Colombo and other major cities
  • Car: About 2-3 hour drive from Colombo via Southern Expressway
