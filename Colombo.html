Colombo

Vibrant capital city blending old and new

Discover Colombo

Colombo is the commercial capital and largest city of Sri Lanka, offering a fascinating mix of colonial architecture, modern skyscrapers, bustling markets, and cultural landmarks. While often treated as just a transit point, Colombo rewards visitors who take time to explore its diverse neighborhoods and rich history.

About Colombo

With a history dating back over 2,000 years, Colombo has been an important port city for centuries, ruled by Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonizers before independence. Today it's a city of contrasts where colonial-era buildings stand alongside modern developments, and where diverse communities live side by side.

Did You Know?

Colombo's name comes from the Sinhalese "Kola-amba-thota" meaning "harbor with leafy mango trees" - a reference to the city's origins as a port surrounded by mango groves.

Things to Do

  • Gangaramaya Temple: Beautiful Buddhist temple complex
  • Galle Face Green: Oceanfront promenade perfect for sunset
  • National Museum: Showcases Sri Lanka's history
  • Pettah Market: Vibrant bazaar with everything imaginable
  • Colombo Fort Area: Colonial architecture and modern offices
  • Seema Malaka Temple: Serene temple on Beira Lake
  • Mount Lavinia Beach: Popular beach just south of city

Best Time to Visit

Colombo can be visited year-round, but the best weather is from January to March when temperatures are slightly cooler and rainfall is lowest. December is festive but crowded. The city experiences two monsoon seasons (May-July and October-November) but showers are usually brief.

Neighborhoods to Explore

Fort: Historic colonial district with modern offices
Pettah: Bustling market area with narrow streets
Galle Face: Oceanfront area with hotels and promenade
Cinnamon Gardens: Upscale residential area with museums
Bambalapitiya: Shopping and dining district
Mount Lavinia: Beach area with relaxed vibe

How to Get Around

Colombo has various transportation options:

  • Tuk-tuks: Most convenient for short distances (use meter or negotiate price first)
  • Ride-hailing: Uber and PickMe apps work well
  • Buses: Extensive network but can be crowded
  • Trains: Good for trips along the coast
  • Walking: Possible in some areas like Galle Face
