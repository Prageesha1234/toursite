Mirissa

Tropical beach paradise and whale watching capital

Discover Mirissa

Mirissa is a small beach town on the south coast of Sri Lanka, known for its palm-fringed golden beaches, relaxed vibe, and as one of the best places in the world for blue whale watching. The crescent-shaped beach and clear waters make it perfect for swimming, surfing, and simply unwinding.

About Mirissa

Once a quiet fishing village, Mirissa has developed into a popular tourist destination while maintaining much of its charm. The main beach is divided by a large rocky outcrop called Parrot Rock, which can be climbed for panoramic views. The west side is more developed with restaurants and bars, while the east side remains quieter.

Did You Know?

Mirissa is one of the few places in the world where you can see blue whales (the largest animals on Earth) just a few kilometers from shore, with sighting rates over 90% in season.

Things to Do

  • Whale watching: See blue whales and dolphins (Nov-Apr)
  • Surfing: Great for beginners at Mirissa Beach
  • Beach hopping: Explore nearby Secret Beach and Coconut Tree Hill
  • Nightlife: Beach bars with fire dancers
  • Diving/Snorkeling: Coral reefs near Polhena
  • Yoga: Several retreats and daily classes

Best Time to Visit

The best time for whale watching is from November to April when the seas are calm. The weather is generally good year-round, though May-October sees more rain. December-January is peak season with higher prices.

Whale Watching Tips

Season: November to April (best Dec-Mar)
Tours: Leave around 6:30am, last 4-6 hours
Cost: Approximately $50-70 per person
Boats: Choose ethical operators with naturalists onboard
What to bring: Sunscreen, hat, seasickness pills if needed

How to Get There

Mirissa is located about 150km south of Colombo. Access options:

  • From Colombo: 3-4 hour drive via Southern Expressway
  • From Galle: 1 hour by bus or train
  • From Ella: 4 hour scenic train to Matara + short tuk-tuk
