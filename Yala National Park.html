Yala National Park

Sri Lanka's premier wildlife sanctuary

Discover Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka, renowned for its high density of leopards and diverse ecosystems. The park covers 979 square kilometers and is divided into five blocks, with only two blocks currently open to the public.

About Yala

Yala combines a strict nature reserve with a national park, featuring varied ecosystems including moist and dry monsoon forests, thorn forests, grasslands, wetlands, and marine environments. The park is home to 44 varieties of mammal and 215 bird species, making it one of the best places in the world to spot leopards in the wild.

Did You Know?

Yala has one of the highest leopard densities in the world, with about 40 leopards roaming Block I (the most visited area of the park).

Wildlife to Spot

  • Leopards (Yala's star attraction)
  • Asian elephants (herds of 10-20 individuals)
  • Sloth bears (best seen February-July)
  • Crocodiles (both mugger and estuarine)
  • Over 200 bird species including endemic species
  • Spotted deer, sambar, wild boar, and water buffalo

Best Time to Visit

The best time for wildlife viewing is during the dry season from February to July when animals congregate around water sources. The park closes annually in September for maintenance.

Safari Information

Safari Times: Morning (6:00-10:00) and afternoon (14:00-18:00)
Duration: 3-4 hours per safari (recommended to do both)
Vehicle: Open-top 4WD jeeps with experienced trackers
Entrance Fee: Approximately $15 per person plus jeep fees

How to Get There

Yala is located in the southeast region of Sri Lanka, about 300km from Colombo. Access points:

  • From Colombo: 6-7 hour drive via Southern Expressway
  • From Galle: 3-4 hour drive
  • From Ella: 3 hour drive
