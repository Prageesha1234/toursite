Horton Plains National Park

Misty highland wilderness with breathtaking views

Discover Horton Plains

Horton Plains National Park is a protected area in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, covering 3,160 hectares of cloud forest and grassland at elevations of 2,100–2,300 meters. The park is famous for its dramatic escarpment known as "World's End" that drops nearly 880 meters, offering spectacular views on clear mornings.

About Horton Plains

This unique ecosystem is home to many endemic species of flora and fauna. The plains were originally named after Sir Robert Wilmot-Horton, a British governor of Ceylon. The park features a beautiful montane grassland interspersed with patches of thick forest, rocky outcrops, and several streams and waterfalls.

Did You Know?

Horton Plains contains the most important catchment area for several of Sri Lanka's major rivers, including the Mahaweli, Kelani, and Walawe.

Key Attractions

  • World's End: Dramatic cliff with 880m drop
  • Baker's Falls: Beautiful 20m waterfall
  • Chimney Pool: Natural rock pool
  • Mini World's End: Smaller viewpoint
  • Unique wildlife: Sambar deer, purple-faced langur
  • Endemic birds: Sri Lanka whistling thrush, yellow-eared bulbul

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is early in the morning (arrive by 6am) when the views from World's End are clearest before clouds roll in by 9-10am. The dry season from December to March offers the best conditions. Temperatures can drop to 5°C, so bring warm clothing.

Trekking Information

Main Trail: 9.5km loop (3-4 hours)
Difficulty: Moderate (some uphill sections)
Entrance Fee: Approximately $15 per person
Guides: Available but not mandatory
Rules: No plastic allowed (strictly enforced)

How to Get There

Horton Plains is located about 30km from Nuwara Eliya. Access options:

  • From Nuwara Eliya: 1.5 hour drive (hire a van/tuk-tuk)
  • From Colombo: 6-7 hour drive via Hatton
  • From Kandy: 4 hour drive via Ginigathena
