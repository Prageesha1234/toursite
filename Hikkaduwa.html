Hikkaduwa

Coral reefs, sea turtles, and beachside bliss

Discover Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa is a vibrant beach town on Sri Lanka's southwest coast, famous for its coral sanctuary, sea turtles, and lively nightlife. The golden sandy beaches and clear waters make it perfect for snorkeling, diving, and simply relaxing under palm trees.

About Hikkaduwa

Once a quiet fishing village, Hikkaduwa became Sri Lanka's first beach resort area in the 1960s. The Hikkaduwa Marine Sanctuary protects a beautiful coral reef just offshore, home to colorful fish and sea turtles. The town has a fun, bohemian vibe with beach bars, restaurants, and shops lining the main road parallel to the beach.

Did You Know?

Hikkaduwa's coral reef is one of the few in Sri Lanka where you can reliably see sea turtles year-round, including the endangered green turtle and hawksbill turtle.

Things to Do

  • Snorkeling/Diving: Explore the coral reef sanctuary
  • Turtle Watching: See turtles feeding in the lagoon
  • Glass Bottom Boat: View marine life without getting wet
  • Surfing: Good waves for beginners to intermediate
  • Beach Hopping: Narigama and Thiranagama beaches nearby
  • Nightlife: Beach parties and fire dancing shows

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Hikkaduwa is from November to April when the weather is dry and seas are calm for water activities. The water visibility is best from January to March. May to October sees more rain but fewer crowds.

Marine Life Information

Coral Reef: Protected marine sanctuary with 60+ coral species
Turtle Species: Green, hawksbill, and occasionally leatherback
Best Viewing: Early morning when turtles come to feed
Snorkeling: Can be done directly from the beach
Ethical Note: Don't touch or feed the turtles

How to Get There

Hikkaduwa is located about 100km south of Colombo. Access options:

  • From Colombo: 1.5-2 hours by train (scenic coastal route)
  • By Car: 2 hours via Southern Expressway
  • From Galle: 30 minutes by train or bus
