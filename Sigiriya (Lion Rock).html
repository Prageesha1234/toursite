Sigiriya (Lion Rock)

The 8th Wonder of the World

Discover Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and palace ruin located in central Sri Lanka. This UNESCO World Heritage Site rises dramatically 200 meters above the surrounding plain, offering breathtaking views and a fascinating glimpse into ancient Sri Lankan civilization.

About Sigiriya

Built in the 5th century by King Kasyapa, Sigiriya served as both a palace and fortress. The complex includes water gardens, frescoes, mirror walls, and the iconic lion's paw entrance. The site demonstrates sophisticated urban planning, engineering, and artistic achievements of ancient Sri Lanka.

Did You Know?

The name Sigiriya comes from "Sihagiri" meaning Lion Rock, referring to the massive lion sculpture that once stood at the entrance. Only the giant paws remain today.

Things to Do

  • Climb to the summit (about 1,200 steps)
  • See the famous Sigiriya frescoes (ancient paintings)
  • Visit the Mirror Wall with ancient graffiti
  • Explore the water gardens at the base
  • See the Lion's Paw entrance
  • Visit the Sigiriya Museum

Best Time to Visit

The best time to climb Sigiriya is early morning (6:30am-9:30am) or late afternoon (3:30pm-6:30pm) to avoid the midday heat. The dry season from May to September offers the clearest views.

How to Get There

Sigiriya is located in the Central Province, about 170km from Colombo. You can reach Sigiriya by:

  • Bus: From Dambulla (1 hour) or Colombo (4-5 hours)
  • Car: 4-5 hour drive from Colombo via Kurunegala
  • Tour: Many organized tours include Sigiriya

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 7:00am to 5:30pm daily
Entrance Fee: Approximately $30 for foreigners
Climbing Time: 1-2 hours up, 1 hour down
Difficulty: Moderate - not recommended for those afraid of heights
