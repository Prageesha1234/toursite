Anuradhapura & Polonnaruwa

Ancient Capitals of Sri Lanka

Discover the Ancient Cities

Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa are two of Sri Lanka's most significant archaeological sites, representing the glorious ancient civilizations of the island. Both are UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcasing impressive ruins, stupas, and Buddha statues from different periods of Sri Lankan history.

About Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura was the first ancient capital of Sri Lanka (4th century BC to 11th century AD) and remains an important sacred city for Buddhists. The sprawling ruins include massive dagobas (stupas), ancient pools, and the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree grown from a cutting of the original Bodhi tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment.

About Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa became the second capital after the fall of Anuradhapura (11th-13th century AD). The ruins here are better preserved and more compact, featuring the famous Gal Vihara (rock temple) with four magnificent Buddha statues carved from a single granite rock face.

Did You Know?

The Sri Maha Bodhi in Anuradhapura is the oldest historically authenticated tree in the world, planted in 288 BC and still thriving today.

Key Sites to Visit

In Anuradhapura:

  • Ruwanwelisaya Stupa
  • Jetavanaramaya Stupa
  • Sri Maha Bodhi (Sacred Bo Tree)
  • Abhayagiri Monastery complex
  • Isurumuniya Rock Temple

In Polonnaruwa:

  • Gal Vihara (Rock Temple)
  • Royal Palace complex
  • Rankoth Vehera (Golden Pinnacle Stupa)
  • Lankatilaka Temple
  • Parakrama Samudra (ancient reservoir)

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit both cities is during the dry season from May to September. Early morning visits are recommended to avoid the midday heat. Full moon days (Poya) are particularly special as locals come to worship.

How to Get There

Anuradhapura: 205km north of Colombo (4-5 hours by car/bus)
Polonnaruwa: 140km northeast of Kandy (3-4 hours by car/bus)
Many visitors combine both sites in a "Cultural Triangle" tour with Sigiriya.

Visitor Information

Entrance Fees: Approximately $25 per site for foreigners
Dress Code: Shoulders and knees must be covered at religious sites
Transport: Bicycles available for rent in Polonnaruwa to explore the sites
