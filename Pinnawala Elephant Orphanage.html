Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Sanctuary for orphaned and injured elephants

Discover Pinnawala

The Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is a captive breeding and conservation facility for wild Asian elephants located in the village of Pinnawala, about 45km from Kandy. Established in 1975, it provides care and protection to orphaned and injured elephants, with over 80 elephants currently residing there.

About the Orphanage

Originally established to care for orphaned elephants found in the wild, Pinnawala has become an important conservation center with a successful breeding program. The elephants roam freely during the day and are taken to the nearby Maha Oya river twice daily for bathing, which is a major highlight for visitors.

Did You Know?

Pinnawala has the largest herd of captive elephants in the world, and was the first to record the birth of an elephant conceived and born in captivity in 1984.

Things to Do

  • Watch the river bath: Elephants bathe at 10am and 2pm daily
  • See feeding time: Baby elephants are bottle-fed at 9:15am, 1:15pm, and 5pm
  • Visit the museum: Learn about elephant conservation
  • Walk with elephants: Some hotels offer early morning walks
  • Adopt an elephant: Support their care through adoption programs
  • Shop for paper: Buy paper made from elephant dung

Best Time to Visit

The orphanage is open year-round, but the best time to visit is during the morning bath (10am) when the light is good for photography. Avoid weekends and public holidays when it's more crowded. The dry season (Jan-Apr) is best as the river may be too high during monsoon.

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 8:30am to 5:30pm daily
Entrance Fee: Approximately $15 for foreigners
Bathing Times: 10:00-12:00 and 14:00-16:00
Feeding Times: See above
Photography: Allowed but respect the animals

How to Get There

Pinnawala is located between Colombo and Kandy. Access options:

  • From Kandy: 1 hour by car or bus (route 644)
  • From Colombo: 2.5 hours via Kegalle
  • By Train: Get off at Rambukkana station (10min tuk-tuk ride)
