Arugam Bay

Surfing paradise on Sri Lanka's east coast

Discover Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a crescent-shaped bay on Sri Lanka's east coast, famous for its consistent surf breaks and laid-back vibe. This former fishing village has become a mecca for surfers from around the world, while still maintaining its authentic Sri Lankan charm.

About Arugam Bay

Located about 320km from Colombo, Arugam Bay offers some of the best right-hand point breaks in the world. The main surf point breaks for over 500 meters on good days. Beyond surfing, the area is known for its beautiful beaches, lagoon safaris, and proximity to Kumana National Park.

Did You Know?

Arugam Bay hosts an annual international surfing competition, attracting top surfers to ride its famous "Main Point" break.

Things to Do

  • Surfing: Main Point, Whiskey Point, and Pottuvil Point
  • Lagoon Safari: Spot crocodiles and birds in the Pottuvil Lagoon
  • Visit Kumana National Park: Less crowded alternative to Yala
  • Beach Hopping: Explore nearby Peanut Farm and Elephant Rock
  • Yoga: Several surf-and-yoga retreats available
  • Local Culture: Visit the nearby Muhudu Maha Viharaya temple

Best Time to Visit

The surf season runs from April to October, with the best waves typically from May to September. The area is quiet from November to March when many businesses close due to monsoon rains.

Surf Information

Main Point: World-class right-hand point break
Difficulty: Intermediate to advanced (beginners can learn at Baby Point)
Surf Schools: Several offer board rental and lessons
Board Rental: Approximately $5-10 per day
Lessons: Around $25-40 for 2-hour private lesson

How to Get There

Arugam Bay is located on Sri Lanka's east coast. Access options:

  • From Colombo: 7-8 hour drive via Ratnapura and Monaragala
  • From Ella: 3-4 hour scenic drive through hill country
  • By Bus: Direct buses from Colombo (10 hours) or Batticaloa
