Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

A sacred Buddhist temple housing the relic of the tooth of Buddha

Discover the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, or Sri Dalada Maligawa, is a Buddhist temple in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is located in the royal palace complex which houses the relic of the tooth of Buddha, a tooth that is venerated by Buddhists. The relic has played an important role in the local politics since ancient times, as it is believed that whoever holds the relic holds the governance of the country.

About the Temple

The temple was built by the Kandyan Kings between 1687 and 1707 to house the sacred tooth relic. The architecture is of unique Kandyan style with a moat, decorative golden fence, and beautifully painted ceilings. The temple complex includes several buildings including the main shrine, Alut Maligawa (new shrine), and the Audience Hall.

The tooth relic is kept in a two-story inner shrine fronted by two large elephant tusks. The relic rests on a solid gold lotus flower, encased in jeweled caskets that sit on a throne. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site as part of the Sacred City of Kandy.

Did You Know?

The sacred tooth relic was brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD, hidden in the hair of Princess Hemamali from India. It has survived numerous attempts to capture and destroy it over the centuries.

Things to Do

  • Witness the daily rituals (pujas) performed at 6:30am, 9:30am, and 6:30pm
  • Visit the temple museum with artifacts related to Buddhism and Kandyan kingdom
  • See the golden canopy built over the relic chamber
  • Explore the beautiful temple architecture and decorations
  • Attend the annual Esala Perahera festival in July/August

Best Time to Visit

The temple can be visited year-round, but the most spectacular time is during the Esala Perahera festival (usually in July or August), when the tooth relic is paraded through the streets on a decorated elephant. Morning hours are less crowded for regular visits.

How to Get There

The temple is located in the heart of Kandy city, about 115km from Colombo. You can reach Kandy by:

  • Train: Scenic 3-hour ride from Colombo (recommended)
  • Bus: Regular buses from Colombo and other major cities
  • Car: About 3-4 hour drive from Colombo via A1 highway

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 5:30am to 8:00pm daily
Puja Times: 5:30-7:00am, 9:30-11:00am, 6:30-8:00pm
Dress Code: Shoulders and knees must be covered (sarongs available for rent)
Entrance Fee: Approximately $10 for foreigners
